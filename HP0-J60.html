Which requirement differentiates dual-domain from dual-path SAS?
A. Dual-path requires active SAS cables.
B. Dual-domain requires less hardware.
C. Dual-domain requires dual-port drives.
D. Dual-path requires dual-port controllers.
Answer: C
Explanation: Read Redundancy in enterprise storage networks using dual-domain SAS
configurations document. One of the key differences between dual-domain and dual-path
architectures is the use of SAS or SATA drives. Dual-port SAS drives can provide the
advantages of complete pathway redundancy in a dual-domain configuration. Customers
seeking lower cost solutions can employ single port SATA drives in a dual-path
configuration.
